🏠 How to Photograph Your Property:

  • Front of house - Stand back to capture entire front
  • Back of house - Include deck/patio if present
  • Left side - Show full side from corner to corner
  • Right side - Show full side from corner to corner
  • Roof angle - Best visible angle showing condition
  • Detached structures - Garage, shed, pool house, etc.
  • Special features - Pool, hot tub, solar panels, etc.

💡 Tip: Take photos during daylight for best results

🚗 How to Photograph Your Vehicle:

  • Front view - Stand directly in front, include license plate
  • Back view - Stand directly behind, include license plate
  • Driver side - Full side view from front to back
  • Passenger side - Full side view from front to back
  • Front corner angle - 3/4 view showing front and one side
  • VIN number - Dashboard or driver door frame
  • Odometer - Current mileage reading

💡 Tip: Park in open area with good lighting

📄 Document Photo Tips:

  • Place document on flat surface
  • Ensure good lighting (no shadows)
  • Capture entire document in frame
  • Make sure text is readable
  • Take photos straight down (not angled)

🚨 How to Document Damage/Claims:

  • Overall damage view - Wide shots showing full extent
  • Each damaged area - Individual close-up photos
  • Multiple angles - Same damage from different perspectives
  • Measurement reference - Include ruler or common object for scale
  • Cause of damage - Tree, water source, impact point, etc.
  • Affected rooms/areas - Interior and exterior damage
  • Exchange forms - Clear photo of all insurance exchange documents
  • Police report - Case number and report if applicable
  • Other party info - License plates, insurance cards if accident

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Take photos before any cleanup or repairs
